Triceps

Triceps Dips

What to do:

  • Position yourself on parallel bars, with hands gripping the bars and arms fully extended
  • Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping them close to your sides, until your upper arms are parallel to the ground
  • Push through your palms to extend your arms and return to the starting position

What to avoid:

  • Avoid shrugging your shoulders or letting them hunch up towards your ears during the exercise
  • Do not let your elbows flare out to the sides as you lower your body

Triceps dips on floors

What to do:

  • Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair or bench, with your hands placed behind you on the floor, fingers pointing forward
  • Keep your legs extended in front of you, or slightly bent for modification
  • Lower body by bending elbows, then push back up

What to avoid:

  • Avoid slouching or rounding your shoulders
  • Avoid fully locking your elbows at the top of the movement

Pushdown

What to do:

  • Stand facing a cable machine with a straight bar or rope attachment at chest level
  • Grip the bar or rope with an overhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart
  • Keep your elbows close to your sides and extend your arms downward, focusing on fully contracting your triceps

What to avoid:

  • Avoid leaning forward excessively
  • Don't use excessive momentum, control the weight and movement to prevent swinging motions

Reverse Push up

What to do:

  • Sit on the floor, place hands behind hips with fingers pointing towards feet.
  • Lift hips off the ground using arms and shoulders
  • Keep core engaged and maintain a straight line from shoulders to heels

What to avoid:

  • Avoid sagging your hips or letting them drop towards the ground
  • Don't strain your neck. Keep your neck aligned with your spine and avoid excessive tension
  • Focus on controlled movements and avoid swinging motions to ensure proper muscle activation

Barbell Triceps Extension

What to do:

  • Lie on a bench with your feet flat on the ground and hold a barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly narrower than shoulder-width apart
  • Extend your arms straight up, directly above your shoulders, keeping your elbows locked
  • Extend arms back up to the starting position

What to avoid:

  • Avoid flaring your elbows out to the sides
  • Avoid using excessive weight that compromises form
