Lats

Behind The Neck Pull up

What to do:

  • Stand under a pull-up bar with a wide overhand grip, hands positioned slightly wider than shoulder-width apart
  • Engage your core and retract your shoulder blades
  • Exhale as you pull your body upward towards the bar, bringing your chest close to the bar while maintaining proper form

What to avoid:

  • Forcing your head forward or straining your neck. Keep your neck in a neutral position
  • Using excessive momentum or swinging your body to complete the pull-up

Lat Pulldown

What to do:

  • Sit on a lat pulldown machine with your knees secured under the pad and grab the wide bar with an overhand grip
  • Keep your back straight, chest lifted, and engage your core
  • Exhale as you pull the bar down towards your chest, driving your elbows down and back, while squeezing your shoulder blades together

What to avoid:

  • Leaning back excessively or using your body to swing the weight
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your shoulders

Dumbell Row

What to do:

  • Place one knee and hand on a flat bench, keeping your back parallel to the ground
  • Hold a dumbbell in your other hand with a neutral grip, arm extended downwards
  • Exhale as you pull the dumbbell upward towards your hip, keeping your elbow close to your body and squeezing your shoulder blade at the top

What to avoid:

  • Rounding your back or allowing your shoulder to rotate forward
  • Jerking or using momentum to lift the weight

Seated-Cable-Row

What to do:

  • Sit on a seated cable row machine with your feet securely placed on the footrests
  • Grab the handles with an overhand grip, keeping your arms fully extended in front of you
  • Exhale as you pull the handles towards your abdomen, driving your elbows back and squeezing your shoulder blades together

What to avoid:

  • Arching your lower back or slouching your shoulders. Maintain proper posture throughout the exercise
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your lower back

Lever T-bar Row

What to do:

  • Position yourself with one knee and one hand on the lever T-bar row machine platform, keeping your back parallel to the ground
  • Grab the handles with an overhand grip, arms fully extended downwards
  • Pull the handles towards your abdomen, squeezing your shoulder blades together

What to avoid:

  • Rounding your back or allowing your shoulder to rotate forward
  • Using momentum or jerking the handles

Shoulder Stretch Behind Back

What to do:

  • Stand with good posture, chest lifted, and shoulders relaxed
  • Reach one arm over your shoulder and down your back, placing the palm of your hand between your shoulder blades
  • Use your other hand to gently pull the elbow of the stretched arm towards the midline of your back for a deeper stretch

What to avoid:

  • Avoid excessive force or jerking movements that could strain or injure your shoulder
  • Do not let your shoulders hunch or roll forward during the stretch
