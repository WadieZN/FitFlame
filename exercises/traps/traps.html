Face Pull

What to do:

  • Attach a rope or handle to a cable machine at chest height and stand facing the machine
  • Grasp the rope with a neutral grip (palms facing each other) and retract your shoulder blades
  • Pull the rope towards your face while keeping your elbows high and outward, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the end of the movement

What to avoid:

  • Avoid using excessive weight or resistance that compromises your form
  • Avoid rounding your shoulders or hunching forward during the exercise

Rear Delt Machine Flys

What to do:

  • Adjust the machine so that the handles are positioned at shoulder height
  • Sit with your chest against the pad, grasp the handles with a neutral grip, and maintain a slight bend in your elbows
  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together as you bring the handles back and outward, focusing on activating your rear deltoid muscles

What to avoid:

  • Do not hunch your shoulders or round your back during the exercise
  • Avoid locking your elbows at the end of the movement or hyperextending them

Half Kneeling High Cable Row Rope

What to do:

  • Set the cable machine at a high position and attach a rope handle to the cable
  • Position yourself in a half-kneeling stance with one knee on the ground and the other foot planted in front
  • Grab the rope handles with an overhand grip, keep your back straight, and pull the handles towards your body, squeezing your shoulder blades together

What to avoid:

  • Avoid using excessive momentum or jerking motions to pull the rope
  • Do not let your shoulders shrug or round forward during the row

Upper Back Stretch

What to do:

  • Stand or sit upright with good posture
  • Interlace your fingers and extend your arms in front of you, palms facing away from your body/li>
  • Gently round your upper back, pushing your arms forward and feeling a stretch between your shoulder blades

What to avoid:

  • Avoid straining or forcing the stretch. It should be gentle and comfortable, not painful
  • Do not hunch or slouch your shoulders during the stretch

Barbell Bent Over Row

What to do:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, slightly bend your knees, and hinge forward at the hips while keeping your back straight
  • Grip the barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart
  • Pull the barbell towards your lower chest, keeping your elbows close to your body and squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement

What to avoid:

  • Avoid rounding your back or allowing it to sag during the exercise
  • Avoid pulling the barbell too high or using your neck and traps to lift the weight
