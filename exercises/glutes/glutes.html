Glutes

Barbell Glute Bridge

What to do:

  • Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart
  • Place a barbell across your hip area, holding it securely with both hands
  • Engage your glutes and core, exhale, and lift your hips off the ground by pushing through your heels until your body forms a straight line from your knees to your shoulders

What to avoid:

  • Hyperextending your lower back at the top of the movement
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your lower back

Bench Glute Flutter Kicks

What to do:

  • Lie face down on a bench, with your hips at the edge and your legs extended straight behind you
  • Engage your glutes and core muscles, and lift your legs slightly off the bench
  • Keeping your legs straight, alternate lifting each leg up and down in a fluttering motion while maintaining control and tension in your glutes

What to avoid:

  • Arching your lower back or straining your neck
  • Using excessive momentum or swinging your legs to lift them higher

Cable Pull Through

What to do:

  • Stand facing away from a cable machine with the cable set to a low position
  • Step forward with one foot and hold the handle between your legs, with your arms fully extended and your hips pushed back
  • Drive your hips forward, squeezing your glutes at the top

What to avoid:

  • Rounding your lower back or leaning forward excessively
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your lower back

Towel Leg Curl

What to do:

  • Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor
  • Place a towel under your feet and lift your hips off the ground, supporting your body with your upper back and shoulders
  • Curl your legs towards your body by sliding your feet along the towel

What to avoid:

  • Placing too much pressure on your neck or upper back
  • Allowing your hips to drop or sag

Cable Hip Extension

What to do:

  • Attach an ankle cuff to a cable machine and secure it around your ankle
  • Stand facing the machine and hold onto a support for balance if needed
  • Keep your core engaged, exhale, and extend your leg backward, squeezing your glutes at the top of the movement

What to avoid:

  • Arching your lower back or leaning forward excessively. Maintain a neutral spine throughout the exercise
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your lower back
