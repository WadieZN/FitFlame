Shoulders

Dumbbell Lateral Raise

What to do:

  • Stand tall, lift dumbbells to shoulder level
  • Engage core, control movement, contract shoulders
  • Maintain form, avoid excessive weight

What to avoid:

  • Swinging or using momentum
  • Engage core, control movement, contract shoulders
  • Using weights that are too heavy

Lateral Raise Machine

What to do:

  • Maintain proper form: Sit upright, grasp the handles, and lift outward, leading with your elbows
  • Start with a manageable weight: Begin with a weight you can handle with good form
  • Control the movement: Perform the exercise slowly and with control

What to avoid:

  • Avoid jerking or swinging the weight
  • Don't lift excessively heavy weights
  • Avoid shrugging your shoulders during the movement

Dumbbell Shoulder Press

What to do:

  • Sit or stand with a straight posture
  • Hold the dumbbells at shoulder level, palms facing forward.
  • Press the dumbbells upward until your arms are fully extended

What to avoid:

  • Avoid arching your back or leaning excessively during the movement
  • Don't lock your elbows at the top of the movement
  • Avoid using momentum or swinging the weights to lift them up

Pike Push up

What to do:

  • Start in a high plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart and your feet hip-width apart
  • Engage your core and maintain a straight line from your head to your heels
  • Lift your hips and walk your feet closer to your hands, creating an inverted V shape with your body

What to avoid:

  • Avoid allowing your lower back to sag or your hips to lift too high
  • Don't let your head drop down or your neck to be strained. Keep your gaze slightly forward
  • Avoid rushing through the exercise

Lever Shoulder Press

What to do:

  • Adjust the seat and lever handles to ensure a comfortable position
  • Sit with proper posture, back against the backrest, and grasp the lever handles
  • Press the handles forward until your arms are fully extended, engaging your shoulder muscles

What to avoid:

  • Avoid arching your back or using excessive momentum during the movement
  • Don't lock your elbows at the top of the movement
  • Avoid using weights that are too heavy, compromising your form and risking injury

Incline Dumbbell Side Lateral Raise

What to do:

  • Sit on an incline bench with your back and head fully supported
  • Hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing inward and arms by your sides
  • Raise the dumbbells out to the sides with a slight bend in your elbows until they reach shoulder level

What to avoid:

  • Avoid using excessive momentum or swinging the weights to lift them up
  • Don't lift the dumbbells too high or let your shoulders shrug upward
  • Avoid leaning back or using your back muscles to lift the weights
