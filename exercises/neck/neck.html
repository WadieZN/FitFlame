Neck

Lying Weighted Lateral Neck Flexion

What to do:

  • Lie on your side and slowly lower your ear towards your shoulder, using a weighted object
  • Maintain control and engage your neck muscles to lift your head back to the starting position
  • Perform the exercise with proper form, avoiding strain on your neck

What to avoid:

  • Avoid using excessive weight.
  • Avoid tensing other muscles, like your jaw or shoulders

Weighted Lying Neck Extension

What to do:

  • Lie on your stomach and slowly lift your head upward, extending your neck, while holding a weighted object
  • Maintain control and focus on engaging the muscles at the back of your neck
  • Perform the exercise with proper form and avoid using excessive weight or forcefully hyperextending your neck

What to avoid:

  • Avoid using excessive weight or straining your neck
  • Avoid forcefully hyperextending your neck to prevent injury

Weighted Lying Neck Flexion

What to do:

  • Lie on your back and slowly lower your head towards your chest while holding a weighted object on your forehead
  • Maintain control and engage the muscles in the front of your neck
  • Perform the exercise with proper form, avoiding excessive weight and sudden movements

What to avoid:

  • Avoid tensing other muscles, such as your jaw or shoulders, during the exercise
  • Avoid using excessive weight or straining your neck

Rotating Neck Stretch

What to do:

  • Start by sitting or standing tall and slowly turn your head to one side, bringing your chin towards your shoulder while keeping your shoulders relaxed
  • Hold the stretch for a few seconds, feeling the gentle stretch along the side of your neck
  • Repeat the stretch on the other side, alternating between both sides

What to avoid:

  • Avoid forcefully twisting or over-rotating your neck, as it can cause strain or injury
  • Avoid holding your breath during the stretch; remember to breathe naturally

Neck Extension Stretching

What to do:

  • Start by sitting or standing tall with good posture
  • Gently tilt your head backward, looking up towards the ceiling, while keeping your shoulders relaxed
  • Hold the stretch for a few seconds, feeling the gentle stretch along the front of your neck

What to avoid:

  • Avoid overextending your neck or forcing the stretch beyond your comfort level
  • Avoid any sudden or jerky movements while performing the stretch
