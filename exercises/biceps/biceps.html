Biceps

Lever Preacher Curl

What to do:

  • Maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight and your elbows stable
  • Engage your biceps by fully contracting them during the curling motion
  • Gradually increase the weight or resistance as your strength improves to continue challenging your muscles.

What to avoid:

  • Avoid using excessive momentum or swinging your body to lift the weight
  • Do not let your elbows drift forward or outward
  • Avoid using weights or resistance that are too heavy

Zottman Curl

What to do:

  • Start with a neutral grip (palms facing inward) during the lifting phase and gradually rotate your wrists to a supinated grip (palms facing upward) during the lowering phase
  • Engage your biceps and forearms throughout the exercise by squeezing them at the top of the curl and controlling the descent
  • Maintain a stable and upright posture, keeping your core engaged and your shoulders back

What to avoid:

  • Swinging your body or using momentum to lift the weight
  • Using weights that are too heavy, compromising form and risking injury

Hammer Curl with Resistance Band

What to do:

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart and maintain good posture throughout the exercise
  • Keep your elbows close to your sides and engage your biceps and forearms while curling
  • Gradually increase the tension of the resistance band as your strength improves to continue challenging your muscles

What to avoid:

  • Avoid arching your back or leaning backward during the curl
  • Do not let your wrists collapse or bend excessively during the movement

Concentration Curl

What to do:

  • Sit on a bench or chair with your legs spread apart, creating a stable base
  • Place your working arm's elbow against the inner thigh of the same side, allowing it to support the weight
  • Keep your back straight, engage your biceps, and perform a controlled curling motion while focusing on squeezing the muscle at the top

What to avoid:

  • Avoid using momentum or swinging your body to lift the weight
  • Do not allow your elbow to drift away from your inner thigh

Barbell Curl

What to do:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and grip the barbell with an underhand grip (palms facing upward), slightly wider than shoulder-width
  • Keep your elbows close to your sides and engage your biceps as you curl the barbell towards your shoulders
  • Maintain proper form by keeping your back straight, core engaged, and focusing on a controlled movement throughout the exercise.

What to avoid:

  • Do not arch your back or lean backward during the curl

Water Bottle Hammer Curl

What to do:

  • Maintain proper form, keeping your back straight, core engaged, and elbows close to your sides while curling the water bottles
  • Control the movement by performing the exercise in a slow and controlled manner
  • Gradually increase weight and repetitions to challenge your muscles and promote strength and growth

What to avoid:

  • Avoid swinging your body or using excessive body movement during the exercise
  • Don't rely on your shoulders or forearms, keep the emphasis on your biceps
