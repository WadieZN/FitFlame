Abdominals

Decline Sit up

What to do:

  • Maintain proper form and engage core muscles
  • Breathe in as you lower your upper body, and exhale as you contract your abs to come up
  • Gradually increase the difficulty by adjusting the bench angle or adding resistance

What to avoid:

  • Swinging or using momentum to complete the movement
  • Straining your neck by pulling on it or leading with your head
  • Overexerting yourself or sacrificing form for more repetitions

Standing Cable Crunch

What to do:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and grab the cable attachment with a neutral grip
  • Engage your core muscles and maintain a tall posture throughout the exercise
  • Exhale as you contract your abdominal muscles and bring your elbows towards your knees

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive weight or momentum to pull the cable down
  • Straining your neck by pulling on the cable or leading with your head
  • Arching your back or leaning excessively forward or backward during the movement

Tuck Crunch

What to do:

  • Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor
  • Bring your knees towards your chest, lifting your shoulder blades off the ground
  • Engage your core and exhale as you contract your abdominal muscles

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive momentum to lift your upper body
  • Pulling on your neck or leading the movement with your head
  • Straining your lower back by arching it excessively during the crunch

Full Crunch Machine

What to do:

  • Sit on the crunch machine with your feet securely anchored under the footpads
  • Place your hands lightly on the side handles or behind your head, without pulling on your neck
  • Engage your core, exhale, and contract your abdominal muscles as you lift your upper body off the backrest

What to avoid:

  • Using momentum to swing your body or relying on the machine to do the work for you
  • Pulling on your neck or placing excessive strain on your cervical spine

Weighted Crunch

What to do:

  • Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor
  • Hold a weight plate or dumbbell against your chest or extend your arms overhead with a weight
  • Engage your core, exhale, and contract your abdominal muscles as you lift your upper body off the floor, bringing your chest towards your knees

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive momentum or swinging your body to complete the crunch
  • Pulling on your neck or leading the movement with your head
