Lower Back

Weighted Back Extension

What to do:

  • Lie face down on a back extension machine with your legs secured and your upper body hanging freely
  • Hold a weight plate against your chest or place it behind your head
  • Engage your lower back and glutes, exhale, and lift your upper body until it aligns with your legs, keeping your back straight

What to avoid:

  • Hyperextending your back or arching too much
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your lower back

Lever Back Extension

What to do:

  • Position yourself on a lever back extension machine with your feet secured and your upper body hanging freely
  • Place your hands behind your head or across your chest
  • Lift your upper body until it aligns with your legs, keeping your back straight

What to avoid:

  • Jerking or using momentum to lift your upper body
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your lower back

T-Bar Row

What to do:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, straddling the T-bar row machine
  • Bend your knees slightly and hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and chest lifted
  • Grip the handles with an overhand grip, palms facing each other, and exhale as you pull the handles towards your abdomen, driving your elbows back and squeezing your shoulder blades together

What to avoid:

  • Rounding your back or allowing your shoulders to slump forward
  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your lower back

Flat Bench Hyperextension

What to do:

  • Lie face down on a flat bench with your hips positioned at the edge and your feet securely anchored
  • Cross your arms in front of your chest or place your hands behind your head
  • Engage your lower back and glutes, exhale, and lift your upper body off the bench until it aligns with your legs, keeping your back straight

What to avoid:

  • Hyperextending your back or arching too much
  • Placing excessive strain on your neck or using your neck to lift your upper body
