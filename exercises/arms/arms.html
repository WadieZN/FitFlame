Forearms

Barbell Reverse Wrist Curl Over a Bench

What to do:

  • Sit on a bench with your forearms resting on the bench, palms facing down and gripping a barbell
  • Keep your wrists in a neutral position, not flexed or extended
  • Slowly curl the barbell upward by flexing your wrists, contracting your forearm muscles

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your wrists
  • Allowing your wrists to bend or extend during the exercise.
  • Jerking or using momentum to lift the barbell

Dumbbell Scott Hammer Curl

What to do:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a pair of dumbbells with an underhand grip (palms facing your body)
  • Keep your upper arms stationary and exhale as you curl the dumbbells upward, focusing on contracting your biceps
  • Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position while inhaling

What to avoid:

  • Straining your back or shoulders by arching or shrugging during the exercise

Barbell Reverse Wrist Curl

What to do:

  • Sit on a bench with your forearms resting on your thighs, palms facing down and gripping a barbell
  • Start with your wrists in a neutral position, not flexed or extended
  • Exhale as you curl the barbell upward by flexing your wrists, contracting your forearm muscles

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your wrists
  • Allowing your wrists to bend or extend during the exercise. Maintain a stable wrist position throughout the movement

Hammer Curl with Resistance Band

What to do:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, placing the resistance band under the arches of your feet
  • Hold the resistance band handles with a neutral grip (palms facing each other)
  • Keeping your elbows close to your sides, exhale as you curl the resistance band upward, contracting your biceps

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive momentum or jerking the resistance band
  • Allowing your elbows to flare out or shoulders to shrug. Keep your upper arms stationary

Dumbbell Seated Neutral Wrist Curl

What to do:

  • Sit on a bench with your feet flat on the floor, holding a pair of dumbbells with a neutral grip
  • Rest your forearms on your thighs or a bench, allowing your wrists to hang off the edge
  • Exhale as you curl the dumbbells upward by flexing your wrists, focusing on contracting your forearm muscles

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or strains your wrists
  • Allowing your wrists to bend or extend during the exercise
