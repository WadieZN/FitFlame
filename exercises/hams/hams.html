Hamstrings

Glute Kickback Machine

What to do:

  • Position yourself on the glute kickback machine with your knees on the padding and your hands holding onto the handles or the machine's frame
  • Engage your core and keep your back straight throughout the movement
  • Kick your leg backward, extending it fully while squeezing your glute muscles at the top of the movement

What to avoid:

  • Avoid arching your back or overarching your spine during the exercise
  • Don't use excessive momentum or swing your leg to kick it back

Stiff Leg Deadlift

What to do:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and a slight bend in your knees
  • Hold a barbell or dumbbells in front of your thighs with an overhand grip
  • Hinge at the hips while maintaining a flat back, slowly lowering the weights down your legs until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings

What to avoid:

  • Avoid rounding your back during the movement. Keep your spine straight and engaged
  • Avoid using your lower back to lift the weights

Standing Hamstring Stretch

What to do:

  • Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart
  • Extend one leg straight in front of you with your toes pointing upward
  • Hinge forward at the hips, reaching towards your toes, until you feel a gentle stretch in the back of your extended leg

What to avoid:

  • Avoid rounding your back during the stretch. Keep your spine straight and elongated
  • Don't force the stretch or bounce. Maintain a static, controlled stretch without any sudden movements

Seated Leg Curl

What to do:

  • Adjust the seat and leg pad to a comfortable position.
  • Sit with proper posture, back against the backrest, and feet securely placed against the leg pad
  • Engage your hamstring muscles and curl your legs, pulling the leg pad toward your glutes

What to avoid:

  • Avoid arching your back or using your lower back to lift the weight. Keep your core engaged and focus on isolating the hamstring muscles
  • Don't use excessive weight that compromises your form or causes discomfort

Barbell Glute Bridge Two Legs on Bench

What to do:

  • Position a bench horizontally and sit on the floor with your back against the bench
  • Place a barbell across your hips, ensuring it is stable and secure
  • With your feet flat on the ground and shoulder-width apart, push through your heels and lift your hips upward, squeezing your glutes at the top

What to avoid:

  • Avoid overarching your back during the movement. Keep your spine aligned to maintain stability
  • Avoid pushing through your toes or letting your knees cave inward
