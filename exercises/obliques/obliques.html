Obliques

Side Plank Oblique Crunch

What to do:

  • Start in a side plank position, with one forearm on the ground and your body forming a straight line from head to feet
  • Lift your top arm and bring it down towards your hip, simultaneously crunching your side waist
  • Engage your core throughout the exercise to maintain stability and control

What to avoid:

  • Rushing the movement or using momentum
  • Allowing your hips to drop or sag

Cable Side Bend

What to do:

  • Stand next to a cable machine with your feet shoulder-width apart and the cable set at a low position
  • Grasp the handle with one hand and keep your arm extended, palm facing inward
  • Slowly bend sideways away from the machine, keeping your core engaged and maintaining a straight posture

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or stability
  • Rounding or hunching your shoulders

Cross Crunch

What to do:

  • Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor
  • Place your hands behind your head, elbows pointing out to the sides
  • Lift your shoulders and upper back off the ground, simultaneously bringing your right elbow towards your left knee, and then return to the starting position. Repeat on the opposite side, bringing your left elbow towards your right knee

What to avoid:

  • Pulling on your neck or using your hands to forcefully lift your upper body
  • Using excessive momentum or swinging motions

Standing Cable High To Low Twist

What to do:

  • Stand facing a cable machine with the cable set at a high position
  • Hold the cable handle with both hands and stand with your feet shoulder-width apart
  • Keep your arms extended and pull the cable down diagonally across your body, twisting your torso as you bring the handle toward the opposite hip

What to avoid:

  • Using excessive weight that compromises your form or stability
  • Over-rotating or twisting too forcefully, which can strain your back or cause imbalance

Oblique Floor Crunches

What to do:

  • Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor
  • Place your hands behind your head or cross them over your chest
  • Lift your shoulders and upper back off the floor, simultaneously twisting your torso to bring your right elbow towards your left knee. Repeat on the opposite side, bringing your left elbow towards your right knee

What to avoid:

  • Pulling on your neck or using your hands to forcefully lift your upper body
  • Straining your neck or shoulders
